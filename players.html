Players

Short profiles of notable players in the modern era, followed by a brief history of the sport.

Ma Long

China · Right-handed shakehand

Often called the greatest of all time. Olympic singles champion in 2016 and 2020, known for consistency and a complete game.

Fan Zhendong

China · Right-handed shakehand

Paris 2024 Olympic singles champion. Explosive backhand and quick adjustments at the table.

Timo Boll

Germany · Left-handed shakehand

The face of European table tennis for two decades. Former world number one with elegant touch.

Sun Yingsha

China · Right-handed shakehand

Paris 2024 Olympic singles champion. A quick-twitch attacker with an excellent short game.

Chen Meng

China · Right-handed shakehand

Tokyo 2020 Olympic singles champion. Known for power, composure, and an aggressive backhand.

Mima Ito

Japan · Right-handed shakehand

Uses short pips on her backhand to disrupt rhythm. One of the most unorthodox top players in the world.

A Short History

Table tennis began in late nineteenth century England as an after-dinner parlor game. The first commercial set was sold under the name "ping-pong" in 1901.

The International Table Tennis Federation was founded in 1926. For decades, European players dominated, until China invested heavily in the sport in the 1960s and began producing generations of world-class players.

Table tennis joined the Olympic program in 1988 and remains one of the most-watched events of the Summer Games.
